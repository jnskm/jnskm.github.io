### Lesson 1: Loyalty Over Party

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-007]** · page 6 · 102w · _confused, striving, trusting_

Not to whoever had the best snack. Not to whoever seemed more fun. Not to the loudest voice. Or the sweetest voice. But to his people. In politics that kind of loyalty is almost exinct. These days, we’re trained to treat loyalty like a party favor. Easy to give. Easy to take back. It shifts with the headlines, bends with the polling numbers, and disappears the moment it’s inconvenient. 6 But the loyalty God calls us to is different. It doesn’t bow to platforms. It doesn’t pledge allegiance to personalities. It stands still — eyes on the door — no matter what.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-008]** · page 7 · 93w · _confused, trusting, called_

In the Bible, we meet people like Daniel who served in a pagan government yet refused to abandon his devotion to God. When the law said “worship the king,” Daniel went home, opened his windows, and prayed anyway. Not as a protest. Not to make a scene. But because he was loyal — to the One who had never left him. We need more Daniels. Not more partisans. Not more tribalists. Not more people addicted to “our side winning.” We need people who stay loyal to God, even when their party forgets Him.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-009]** · page 7 · 113w · _confused, striving, trusting, surrendered_

Let’s be honest — no political party owns the truth. No candidate is perfect. Far from. Every human system is flawed. That’s why God didn’t ask us to pledge allegiance to a governmetn system or to a party. He commanded us to follow Him. To be holy. To love mercy. To act justly. To walk humbly. That kind of loyalty is hard, in fact impossible without God. But it’s the only kind that lasts. 7 Just like that dog at the door — still, quiet, focused — the world may not understand why we don’t jump at every command or chase every treat. But we’re not here for applause. We’re here for faithfulness.

### Lesson 2: Barking For The Right Reasons

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-012]** · page 10 · 84w · _proud, striving, called_

It was the kind of bark that meant something. She wasn’t afraid. She wasn’t panicking. She just knew what was right. We need that kind of bark today. The book of Proverbs says, “A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger.” (Proverbs 15:1) And yet, many of us have gotten used to harsh words. Loud tweets. Angry headlines. Pretend rage dressed up as passion for justice. But yelling isn’t the same as righteousness. And barking doesn’t mean you’re right.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-013]** · page 10 · 90w · _proud, striving, called_

Jesus didn’t waste words. He spoke clearly, truthfully — and always for the right reasons. When He overturned the tables in the temple, it wasn’t out of outrage addiction. It was holy zeal. It was love defending holiness. That’s the difference. 10 Dogs don’t bark for attention. They bark because they care. They bark because something isn’t right. They bark because someone they love might be in danger. So ask yourself: When I speak up, am I doing it out of love or pride? Is it conviction — or ego?

### Lesson 3: Guarding the Gates

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-015]** · page 12 · 96w · _called, discouraged, afraid_

If danger came, the watchman would sound the alarm. If danger came and he stayed silent, people would get hurt. If he spoke up, even if no one listened, he had done his part. Some of us have stopped watching. 12 We think someone else will guard the gate. Someone smarter. Louder. More important. But what if God placed you where you are for a reason? You don’t have to guard everything. But you do need to stay alert. In your home. Your school. Your church. Watch what comes in. And pay attention to what lingers.

### Lesson 4: Knowing The Master's Voice — *Don't follow the loudest voice — follow the true voice. A political parallel about not being swayed by charisma.*

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-017]** · page 14 · 80w · _confused, trusting, waiting_

Jesus once said, “My sheep listen to my voice; I know them, and they follow me.” (John 10:27) He was talking about us. He wanted us to know Him well enough that when He speaks — we recognize it. And when He doesn’t — we wait. 14 In politics, this is hard. Every side says they speak for truth. Every leader says they care. But the more we listen to the crowd, the harder it is to hear the Shepherd.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-018]** · page 15 · 91w · _confused, trusting, resting, waiting_

So how do we tell the difference? A stranger’s voice will always pull you away. God’s voice draws you near. The stranger rushes. Pressures. Flatters. The Shepherd leads. Gently. Firmly. Always with love. You don’t need to know every answer. But you do need to know the One who does. And that means spending time with Him. Listening in to His voice, quietly. Reading His words. Chewing on the meaning. Letting the Holy Spirit shape your heart. The better you know His voice, the less tempted you’ll be to follow another.

### Lesson 5: Don't Chase Every Squirrel — *Distraction is the enemy of discernment. Politicians thrive on distraction; Christians must stay focused.*

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-020]** · page 16 · 89w · _anxious, striving, weary_

We argue. We worry. We pick sides. And before long, we’ve forgotten what we were supposed to be doing in the first place. The Bible says, “Set your minds on things above, not on earthly things.” (Colossians 3:2) That’s hard to do when you’re sprinting after every distraction. But staying focused doesn’t mean you don’t care. It means you care enough not to waste your energy on noise. 16 Dogs chase squirrels because it’s instinct. People chase them because it feels like action; we feel like we’re doing something.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-021]** · page 17 · 87w · _anxious, striving, resting, trusting_

But real wisdom isn’t about reacting to everything. It’s about knowing what matters — and letting the rest go. Jesus wasn’t easily distracted. When people tried to trap Him with clever questions or emotional arguments, He didn’t take the bait. He saw right through the noise and spoke to the heart of the issue. Every time. You don’t have to respond to everything. You don’t have to fix every argument. And you definitely don’t have to chase every squirrel. Some things are better left in the tree.

### Lesson 6: Don't Roll In The Mud — *Engage, but don't get filthy. A lesson in clean confrontation — truth with grace.*

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-023]** · page 18 · 92w · _tempted, ashamed, called_

But the moment you roll in the mud, you lose more than your position — you lose your integrity, your witness. 18 The apostle Paul once wrote, “Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good.” (Romans 12:21) That’s not a slogan. That’s a command. The world already has enough mud. What it needs are people willing to stay clean — even when it’s messy. That doesn’t mean you need to always stay quiet. It doesn’t mean you ignore injustice. It means you answer with truth — not with tricks.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-024]** · page 19 · 71w · _tempted, ashamed, trusting, called_

Jesus could’ve out-argued every Pharisee. Easily. But He never stooped to their level. He didn’t roll in their mud. He humbly and gently stood firm in the light of truth. When the world plays dirty, shine brighter. When politics gets ugly, speak with grace. You don’t have to match the mess to make a difference. You just have to stay clean — even if you’re the only one who does. 19

### Lesson 7: Be Watchful, Not Fearful — *There's a difference between vigilance and paranoia. A good dog does not panic — it watches and waits.*

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-026]** · page 20 · 91w · _anxious, afraid, trusting, resting_

But choices made out of fear are almost always bad choices. It rushes. It exaggerates. It forgets that God is still in charge. He has always been and will always be. Jesus once told His followers to stay awake — to be ready. But He never told them to panic. In fact, again and again, He said, “Do not be afraid.” 20 We were never meant to live with our hearts clenched. Being faithful doesn’t mean being frightened. Quite the opposite: it means keeping your eyes open and your feet steady.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-027]** · page 21 · 52w · _anxious, afraid, trusting, resting_

Like a good dog watching the gate — not pacing, not growling, just… watching. Trust that when it’s time to act, the Master will let you know. And until then, you can sit quietly. Because you’re not guarding the world on your own. You’re just doing your part. And that’s enough. 21

### Lesson 8: Who Is Holding The Leash?

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-029]** · page 22 · 94w · _proud, striving, surrendered_

And sometimes, we do the same. We say we’re serving God, but deep down we’re really serving ourselves. We want approval. Or control. Or just the thrill of being important and everyone listening to you. 22 But the leash tells the truth. In the end, someone is guiding you — shaping your choices, steering your reactions, leading your heart. Jesus said, “No one can serve two masters.” (Matthew 6:24) You can’t follow God and follow your ego. You can’t walk in truth and be led by fear. Sooner or later, one will pull harder.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-030]** · page 23 · 65w · _striving, trusting, surrendered, resting_

That’s why it matters who’s holding the leash. Let it be the One who loves you. The One who doesn’t yank you or trick you or push you. The One who walks beside you and knows where you’re suppose to be going — even when you have no clue. Because when God leads, you don’t have to run wild. You just have to follow. 23

### Lesson 9: When To Sit And When To Stand

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-032]** · page 24 · 118w · _confused, trusting, called_

There are times in life when obedience means sitting still. But there are also times when faithfulness means standing up. 24 In politics, people argue about when to resist and when to comply. Some shout at every little thing. Others stay silent, even when something deeply wrong is happening. But wisdom is not about picking a side. Wisdom is about listening to God. Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego didn’t stand up to everything. They served a foreign king. They did their work. But when they were told to bow to a false god, they stood. Quietly and calmly. It was not about hate, but about love for God. They knew who they served. And that made the choice simple.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-033]** · page 25 · 72w · _confused, waiting, trusting, called_

Sometimes faith looks like waiting patiently. Other times it looks like refusing to move. The difference isn’t pride. It’s purpose. A dog doesn’t stand to prove something. He stands to protect. So before you act, ask: “Is this about me — or about what’s right?” If you walk closely with the Lord, you’ll know when to sit. And when it’s time to stand. You won’t need anyone’s permission. You’ll just know. 25

### Lesson 10: Be A Shepherd, Not A Politician — *Care for the flock — don't just court votes. Pastoral responsibility over political ambition.*

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-035]** · page 26 · 112w · _called, loved, trusting_

Jesus said, “The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep.” (John 10:11) Was Jesus talking about a job? No. He was talking about love. A love that stems from deep within the heart. A love that is steady, watchful, protective. The kind of love that doesn’t quit when it gets hard. 26 Too many leaders today chase approval instead of people. They run campaigns, not care. They protect their positions, not the vulnerable. But shepherding doesn’t need a title. You don’t have to be famous, have lots of important-sounding titles or degrees to watch over the ones near you. You just need to notice them. And stay close to them.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-036]** · page 27 · 45w · _called, discouraged, trusting_

The best dogs never look like they’re doing much. But they’re always aware. Always ready. Always nearby. You don’t have to win arguments or gather votes to make a difference. Just look after the ones God gave you. That’s what a good shepherd does. 27

### Lesson 11: Wagging Tails And Wounded Hearts

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-037]** · page 28 · 87w · _lonely, loved, forgotten_

He greeted everyone with a wag. Didn’t matter who you were, or how your day had gone. If you walked through that door, you were met with kindness. That wag wasn’t just excitement. Yes, he was excited, but it was also a welcome. There’s something healing about being seen, welcomed — not for what you’ve done, not for what you believe, not for how smart you are or how much you have, but simply because you’re human. Dogs seem to understand that much better than we do.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-038]** · page 28 · 111w · _lonely, ashamed, loved, weary_

Politics, on the other hand, tends to sort people into camps: Us versus Them. Right versus. Wrong. Blue versus. Red. Win versus Lose. But people aren’t sides. People are souls, precious souls. And many of them are wounded. 28 They’ve been hurt by words, or the lack of words, left out of conversations, judged by labels. Some… many don’t trust anymore. And some are just tired. When Jesus walked the earth, He didn’t check credentials before healing. He didn’t quiz people on what they understood. He didn’t require that you be on this side or that side. He saw the heart first. He saw the wound — and moved toward it.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-039]** · page 29 · 74w · _lonely, weary, loved, hopeful_

That’s what mercy does. It doesn’t mean we ignore the truth. It means we carry the truth but with gentleness and humility. The world doesn’t need more shouting. It needs more wagging — small signs of welcome. A look that says, “You matter.” A tone that says, “You’re safe here, even if we disagree.” Kindness won’t fix everything. But it opens the door. And sometimes, that’s all we need for healing to begin. 29

### Lesson 12: Faithful In The Fog — *God's truth is still truth, even when visibility is low.*

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-041]** · page 30 · 92w · _confused, afraid, trusting, waiting_

It’s hard to know who to trust when you can’t see what’s next. But God isn’t lost in the fog. He’s still guiding us, and the entire universe. We may not feel Him, but God is still near. And as He has always been, He is still good, and good to us. The Bible says, “We walk by faith, not by sight.” (2 Corinthians 5:7) That isn’t just about believing big things — it’s about trusting God for all the small things in our lives, especially when the way forward isn’t clear.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-042]** · page 30 · 90w · _confused, anxious, waiting, trusting_

30 You don’t need to see everything to stay faithful. Often it’s just the opposite: our faith grows when we don’t see much at all. You just need to know Who you’re walking with. Or Who is walking with us. Sometimes that means standing still. Sometimes that means taking one small, careful step. But always, it means listening for His voice. The world will try to rush you. Push you. Confuse you. But you don’t have to move until the Shepherd puts out His hand for yours and says, “Come.”

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-043]** · page 31 · 95w · _anxious, forgotten, waiting, trusting_

So wait in the fog if you have to. Not with fear, but with faith. Because He hasn’t forgotten you. And He’s never late. 31 Final Thoughts Politics can make us feel like we always have to pick a side, raise our voice, or brace for a fight. But maybe that’s not the only way. Maybe we don’t have to match the noise. Maybe we don’t have to chase every issue, prove every point, or win every argument. Maybe what the world really needs — what God is asking of us — is something simpler.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-044]** · page 32 · 94w · _weary, confused, trusting, called_

Loyalty that doesn’t shift with the headlines. Kindness that continues, even when people disagree. A voice that speaks truth, in love, and onlywhen it’s needed. And the courage to stand still when the fog is thick. We’ve learned a lot from dogs in these pages. They don’t pretend to know everything. They’re not experts or influencers. But they know how to stay close by. They know how to listen, carefully. They know how to wait, sometimes for a long time. They know how to be welcoming. And some it seems know how to love.

---

**[03-one-voice-one-leash-one-lord-045]** · page 32 · 107w · _weary, anxious, trusting, resting_

They guard what matters. They don’t give up when things get tough. And they’re don’t distracted by the crowd. Well, the good ones anyway. That’s what we’re called to be — faithful. Not loud, but quiet and steady. Not driven by fear, but filled by His love and led by the One who sees clearly, even when we can’t. 32 So if you ever feel overwhelmed by the state of the world, take a breath. Look up. Remember who you belong to. You don’t have to carry it all. You can’t, even if you tried. But you can be faithful with what’s right in front of you.
