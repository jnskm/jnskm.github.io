### Lesson 2: The Cold Doesn't Care — *When the world is indifferent, God draws near.*

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-011]** · page 8 · 80w · _afraid, weary, lonely, loved_

The cold doesn’t care who you are. The cold affects everyone and doesn’t care if you were once a business owner, a teacher, a nurse, someone who worked for a big company, or a child. It wraps around anyone it finds—without kindness or memory. But God is not like the cold. “He will cover you with His feathers, and under His wings you will find refuge.” — Psalm 91:4 When the cold starts to freeze your veins, God comes near.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-013]** · page 9 · 88w · _lonely, grieving, forgotten, loved_

This verse isn’t just about clothes. It’s about seeing people — the way Jesus sees them — especially when the world turns away. Maybe you know what it feels like to be cold. Not just your skin, but your soul. Maybe life has numbed you. Maybe you’ve been brushed past or spoken over. Maybe you’re still trying to warm up after a long winter of loss. Please know this: “The Lord is close to the brokenhearted.” — Psalm 34:18 The cold may not care. But the Shepherd does.

### Lesson 3: Someone Stopped for Me

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-017]** · page 11 · 85w · _forgotten, lonely, discouraged, loved_

You might be reading this and feel like no one sees you. Like you’ve been passed by more times than you can count. Maybe your life feels like a mess. Maybe you’ve been hurt by people who were supposed to help. Please hear this: God has never walked past you. Not once. He sees you. He stops. And He stays. 11 And if you’ve been the one to stop — maybe in a small way, maybe with tears in your own eyes — thank you.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-018]** · page 12 · 65w · _lonely, forgotten, called, hopeful_

Every time we pause for someone in pain, every time we sit and wait without judgment, every time we let our hearts be interrupted, we echo the heart of Jesus. Some dogs survive because someone stopped. Some people do, too. And maybe that’s part of how the Shepherd works. Through the slow hands of those who are willing to stop. To listen. To love. 12

### Lesson 4: Left Behind, Not Forgotten

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-021]** · page 13 · 98w · _lonely, grieving, forgotten, loved_

13 But here’s what Scripture says: “Though my father and mother forsake me, the Lord will receive me.” — Psalm 27:10 When everyone else walks away, God does not. God draws closer. He does not leave when things get hard. He does not pull back when we are messed up, weak, or not who we used to be. He stays, close to us. And He carries us through. “Even to your old age and gray hairs I am He, I am He who will sustain you. I have made you and I will carry you.” — Isaiah 46:4

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-022]** · page 14 · 103w · _forgotten, lonely, grieving, restored_

Maybe you’ve been left behind. Maybe by family, friends, or by your church. Maybe by a system that should have helped. Maybe by the version of life you once hoped for. But remember, you’re not forgotten. Not to God. He sees where you are hurting. He knows what you’ve lost. And He’s not waiting for you to be better. He moves toward you. Actually, He’s already here. God is saying to us, “Come to me. Come home.” God’s door is always open. All we have to do is open our own hearts, walk through, and be embraced by God. And we are home.

### Lesson 5: Sharing A Crust

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-025]** · page 15 · 84w · _weary, discouraged, trusting, hopeful_

“She gave more than all the others.” — Luke 21:3 15 Why? Because she gave from her need. Not her extra. She gave out of love. There’s a myth we sometimes believe — that we need to have more than what we need before we can give. More money than we need. More time than we need. More security than we need. But the heart of God often works through what seems not much at all. “Five loaves and two fish.” — John 6:9

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-026]** · page 16 · 98w · _weary, discouraged, called, grateful_

That’s all one boy had. And it was more than enough for thousands — when placed in the hands of Jesus. Maybe all you have today is half a sandwich. Maybe just a kind word. Maybe a quiet moment to sit beside someone who is struggling, in pain, and suffering. It might not feel like much. But in a world where people are passed over who have nothing, even crumbs can taste like a feast. “Whoever gives even a cup of cold water to one of these little ones… will surely not lose their reward.” — Matthew 10:42

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-027]** · page 16 · 57w · _weary, striving, surrendered, trusting_

16 A man used to say, “You’re never too poor to give something. Even if it’s just your place in line.” Because sometimes the greatest generosity isn’t what we give — but how we give it. Give without fear. Give without keeping score. Give without waiting to have more. With open hands. And an open heart. 17

### Lesson 6: He Will Be Back

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-029]** · page 18 · 88w · _waiting, hopeful, lonely, trusting_

That was years ago. But he still sat there — every afternoon — just in case. Faith doesn’t always look bold. Sometimes it looks like sitting, still. Like hoping when everyone else tells you to give up. Like believing love remembers you — even when you’ve been left in silence. 18 Jesus told a story about that kind of waiting. “But while he was still a long way off, his father saw him and was filled with compassion for him; he ran to his son…” — Luke 15:20

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-030]** · page 19 · 112w · _waiting, lonely, hopeful, loved_

The prodigal son had wandered far. But the father had never stopped watching the road. That’s what love does. It waits. It looks out the window. It keeps a light on. There’s a deeper kind of waiting, too. The kind that says: “Even if the person I long for never returns… I will still be ready to forgive. To welcome. To try again.” A woman once told us, “I’ve waited my whole life for someone to come back. But somewhere along the way, I realized… it’s God who’s been waiting for me.” That shift in perspective, toward the truth of God, the heart of God — that quiet awakening — changed her.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-031]** · page 19 · 80w · _waiting, lonely, hopeful, trusting_

Not all waiting ends with a reunion. But all waiting can lead us back to the One who never stopped hoping for us. “The Lord is good to those who wait for Him, to the soul who seeks Him.” — Lamentations 3:25 19 The little dog on the side of the road hadn’t moved in hours. People said he’d been sitting there since morning. Someone must have promised to come back. And maybe they didn’t. But that dog still believed.

### Lesson 7: Fur and Flesh

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-033]** · page 21 · 101w · _ashamed, forgotten, lonely, loved_

Some people only see the outside. The tangled hair. Rips in the clothes. Or the broken places. They see what’s missing. What’s wrong. What doesn’t look clean or safe or presentable. But God sees deeper. “The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart.” — 1 Samuel 16:7 21 There was a man who had lost most of his teeth. His skin was worn, and his eyes were tired. People avoided him in public. He told us, “Sometimes I go days without hearing my own name.”

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-034]** · page 22 · 91w · _ashamed, lonely, forgotten, loved_

But one afternoon, a child came in with a meal for the shelter. And that child looked him in the eyes, smiled wide, and said, “Hi.” That child had no fear. He had no hesitation. All he had was kindness. He said later, “That one moment reminded me I’m still a person.” Jesus did that all the time. He touched lepers. He dined with outcasts. He called people by name when others called them unclean. He never saw people as problems to be fixed — but as souls to be loved.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-035]** · page 22 · 89w · _ashamed, forgotten, loved, restored_

“You knit me together in my mother’s womb… I am fearfully and wonderfully made.” — Psalm 139:13–14 We are not accidents. We are not our bruises. We are not what we’ve lost. We are not less human because we hurt. That black dog didn’t wag his tail for almost a week. He just watched. Waited. Listened. But the first time someone said, “You’re safe here,” something seemed to have changed. He curled up that night, not just because he was tired, but because he finally believed — he belonged.

### Lesson 8: A Bowl of Water and a Name

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-037]** · page 24 · 80w · _lonely, forgotten, loved, hopeful_

A volunteer at a shelter once made it a habit to learn everyone’s name. Even if someone had only been there one night, even if they didn’t want to talk, she’d remember. One man said, “You know what makes this place feel different? They don’t just serve soup. They remember and say my name.” We were never meant to live as nameless bodies moving through the world. We were created to be known — by God and by one another.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-038]** · page 24 · 89w · _forgotten, lonely, loved, hopeful_

“Fear not, for I have redeemed you; I have called you by name; you are mine.” — Isaiah 43:1 24 There is power in being called by your name. In being reminded that you are not invisible. Not forgotten. Not “just another homeless man.” Not “just a stray.” But someone. Someone worth remembering. One day Jesus passed through a crowd and stopped beneath a tree. There was a man hiding there — a tax collector named Zacchaeus, hated and avoided by many. But Jesus didn’t scold him. He said:

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-039]** · page 25 · 89w · _forgotten, lonely, ashamed, loved_

“Zacchaeus, come down immediately. I must stay at your house today.” — Luke 19:5 He said his name. That was the beginning of Zacchaeus’ healing. Not the end of the story — but the start of coming home to God. Maybe you’ve gone a long time without hearing your name in a gentle voice. Maybe you’ve been called things that have hurt you. Maybe you’ve been overlooked, passed by, or treated like you don’t matter. But the Shepherd knows your name. And even now He is calling your name.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-040]** · page 25 · 82w · _lonely, forgotten, loved, called_

Sometimes it’s not the big gestures that carry the most weight. It’s a bowl of water on a hot day. It’s someone remembering you like you matter. It’s being called “friend,” or “sir,” or “ma’am,” when the world expects no one to look you in the eye. 25 “And if anyone gives even a cup of cold water to one of these little ones because he is My disciple, truly I tell you, he will never lose his reward.” — Matthew 10:42

### Lesson 9: No Collar, Still Beloved — *We don't need tags to be claimed by grace.*

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-043]** · page 27 · 124w · _weary, forgotten, ashamed, loved_

We live in a world that often ties worth to paperwork. To housing status. To appearance. To whether we have a place to return to. But grace doesn’t require credentials. Jesus never said, “Show Me your résumé.” He said: “Come to Me, all who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest.” — Matthew 11:28 27 All. No collar required. There was a man who spent months trying to get his ID back. He’d lost it in the shuffle — a shelter here, a hospital there, a bag stolen while he was asleep on the sidewalk. He told us, “It’s like the world forgot I exist.” But then someone called him by name. Someone said, “You’re on the list.” And he wept.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-044]** · page 28 · 116w · _forgotten, lonely, loved, restored_

Not because of the paperwork. But because someone still saw him as someone worth counting. “I will not forget you. See, I have engraved you on the palms of My hands.” — Isaiah 49:15–16 Even when the world loses track of us, God does not. Even if we’re undocumented, displaced, or unnamed, He knows exactly where we are — and to Him, we are not missing. We are beloved. The little dog with no collar? She never left. She didn’t need an owner’s permission to be accepted. She just needed someone to care. And now, years later, she still sleeps on the welcome mat at the church door. Because someone made space. And someone welcomed her.

### Lesson 10: Every Step Counts

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-046]** · page 29 · 88w · _weary, discouraged, hopeful, trusting_

There’s something about steps that keep going even when they hurt. Something holy about a journey that no one else sees — but God does. One man said, “I used to think I had to clean myself up before I could be worth anything again. But now I think — maybe the fact that I’m still walking is the miracle.” Still walking. It is a miracle indeed. Sometimes we think healing has to be quick. That faith has to look strong. That only progress counts if it’s measurable.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-047]** · page 29 · 98w · _weary, discouraged, hopeful, loved_

29 But the Shepherd sees every step. Even the shaky ones. Even the ones that double back. Even the ones where we fall. “The steps of a good man are ordered by the Lord, and He delights in his way. Though he fall, he shall not be utterly cast down, for the Lord upholds him with His hand.” — Psalm 37:23–24 You might not be where you hoped you’d be by now. You might have taken more wrong turns than you can count. But if you’re still trying — still walking — God hasn’t given up on you.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-048]** · page 30 · 103w · _ashamed, weary, discouraged, loved_

The prodigal son didn’t run home. He limped. He had dust on his feet, shame on his shoulders, a speech in his mouth he never had to finish. Because even though he was still far away, his father had already run out to meet him. “While he was still a long way off…” — Luke 15:20 God doesn’t wait at the finish line. He meets us on the road. 30 That old dog eventually stopped walking. But not because he gave up. He stopped because someone opened their door. They said, “You’ve been looking for something. You don’t have to anymore. You’re home.”

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-049]** · page 31 · 56w · _weary, discouraged, hopeful, trusting_

And for the first time in a long time, he lay down for the night without needing to go anywhere tomorrow. Every step you’ve taken — yes, even the wrong ones — have brought you here. And He’s still walking with you. Every mile. Every moment. You are not lost. You are on your way. 31

### Lesson 11: He Found Me First

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-051]** · page 32 · 93w · _lost, lonely, forgotten, loved_

We often talk about “finding God.” As if we’ve been out searching for Him. But the truth is — we were the ones behind the dumpster. And He was the one who came looking. “The Son of Man came to seek and to save the lost.” — Luke 19:10 A man at a shelter once said, “I didn’t walk into that church looking for anything. I just wanted to warm up. But now I know — it was God who brought me there. He found me long before I knew I was missing.”

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-052]** · page 32 · 95w · _forgotten, lonely, hopeful, loved_

32 Jesus told a story about a shepherd. One sheep had wandered away. Just one. But the shepherd didn’t say, “Ninety-nine is good enough.” He left the others and went after the one that was lost. “If a man has a hundred sheep and one of them gets lost, what will he do? Won’t he leave the ninety-nine others in the wilderness and go to search for the one that is lost until he finds it?” — Luke 15:4 Not if he finds it. Until. Because that’s who God is. He does not stop searching.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-053]** · page 33 · 122w · _ashamed, forgotten, hopeful, loved_

Maybe you didn’t come looking for Him today. Maybe you’ve spent years feeling like you’re beyond reach, beyond grace, beyond the kind of rescue songs are written about. But the Shepherd sees where you are. And He’s already walking your way. “I will answer them before they even call to me. While they are still talking about their needs, I will go ahead and answer their prayers!” — Isaiah 65:24 That dog behind the dumpster never asked to be seen. He didn’t have the courage. He didn’t even know how to ask. But someone saw him anyway. Someone leaned down, came down to where he was. And that’s how healing began. Not because he went looking. But because love found him first.

### Lesson 12: The Shepherd Is Our Home — *Real home is not a place — it's a Person.*

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-055]** · page 34 · 96w · _lonely, weary, hopeful, resting_

She found what she’d been looking for. Safety. Food. Consistency. Care. In other words — home. We often think of “home” as a roof and four walls. And it can be. But there’s another kind of home. The kind we carry. The kind we find when we are known, loved, and not sent away. That kind of home doesn’t depend on real estate. It depends on relationships. 34 “Surely Your goodness and mercy will follow me all the days of my life, and I will dwell in the house of the Lord forever.” — Psalm 23:6

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-056]** · page 35 · 83w · _lonely, forgotten, loved, restored_

The house of the Lord is not a place you visit. It’s a place where you are invited to stay, forever. Not because you earned it. Not because you’ve fixed everything about yourself and your life. But because the Shepherd lives there — and He wants you with Him. A guests at a shelter once said, “I’ve lived under porches and overpasses. But the first time someone remembered my name two weeks in a row… that’s when I felt like I’d come home.”

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-057]** · page 35 · 98w · _lonely, weary, hopeful, loved_

He smiled. Then added, “I think God might’ve been following me this whole time.” Jesus didn’t come to give us only shelter. He came to give us Himself. “Remain in me, and I will remain in you.” — John 15:4 Abide. Remain. Stay. Dwell. That’s home. 35 Maybe you’re still searching. Maybe your life has been filled with too many goodbyes, too many doors that didn’t open, too many nights alone. But the Shepherd never left. He’s been walking beside you, even when you didn’t know it. And today, He’s not just offering a place. He’s offering Himself.

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-059]** · page 37 · 92w · _forgotten, lonely, hopeful, loved_

We’ve told stories about good but hurting dogs. But they were never the main character. They were there to remind us of something greater — of Someone greater. Because if a dog can wait at the door for someone who might never return, how much more does God wait for us? If a stray can offer the last crust to a friend, how much more does the Shepherd give? If a nameless dog with no collar can be welcomed with a bowl of water, how much more are you seen? Known? Loved?

---

**[05-without-a-place-not-without-a-shepherd-060]** · page 37 · 91w · _lonely, forgotten, weary, loved_

“I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down His life for the sheep.” — John 10:11 37 You don’t have to earn your way into His flock. You can’t. You don’t have to fix everything first. You can’t. You don’t even have to know where you’re going. Because He knows. And He’s already moving towards you. Actually, He’s right there beside you. If today you’re without a place, please know — you are not without a Shepherd. You are not forgotten. You are not alone. Not now. Not ever.
