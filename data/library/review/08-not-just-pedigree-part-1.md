### Introduction

**[08-not-just-pedigree-part-1-007]** · page 8 · 89w · _loved, striving, ashamed, forgiven_

We’ve learned that dogs don’t read pedigree papers. They don’t care about bloodlines or breeding certificates. A shelter mutt will love you just as fiercely as a champion show dog. And perhaps more importantly, they teach us that worth isn’t determined by lineage. In the same way, God doesn’t check our family tree before offering His love. He doesn’t measure our value by how well we fulfill family expectations or cultural traditions. His love isn’t conditional on whether we become what our parents hoped or what our heritage demands.

### Lesson 1: The Perfect Playdate Pup — *The Pressure of Being "On" All the Time*

**[08-not-just-pedigree-part-1-018]** · page 16 · 92w · _striving, loved, resting, joyful_

His body remembered what it felt like to move without thinking. He ran simply for the sake of running. He missed the ball and did not care. He was clumsy and joyful and completely, naturally himself. Watching him, the girl remembered something from the Bible. When the prophet Samuel went to choose Israel’s next king, he looked at the tall, impressive sons of Jesse and thought surely one of them must be God’s choice. But God told Samuel that people look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart.

The girl understood then what she was seeing in her dog. Visitors judged him by his perfect behavior during their stay, by how well he performed and how impressive he appeared. ### Lesson 2: The Expected Shepherd — *Identity Crisis: "Am I Called or Just Expected?"*

**[08-not-just-pedigree-part-1-029]** · page 23 · 86w · _striving, called, discouraged, hopeful_

The dog’s father’s statue still stood at the training facility entrance. People still spoke respectfully about the legendary bloodline.
