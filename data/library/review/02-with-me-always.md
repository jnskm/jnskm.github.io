### Lesson 1: Sit Still

**[02-with-me-always-007]** · page 5 · 93w · _anxious, striving, weary, resting_

Good dogs know how to sit. They don’t rush things. They don’t panic; well most don’t. They just wait. And when they’re told to sit, they usually do. Not always right away. Sometimes they squirm a little. But in the end, they stay. Oddly enough, that, for a lot of us human beings, is really hard to do. When life gets hard, we want to fix it. We want to do something about it, anything, to make it better. But maybe sometimes, what God wants from us is something quite different, much smaller.

---

**[02-with-me-always-009]** · page 6 · 115w · _weary, anxious, waiting, trusting_

He looked up, smiled, and answered, “Waiting on the Lord. He’s almost never early. But He’s never late either.” He wasn’t doing nothing. He was waiting, with hope. He was still, but not because he had given up. Because he had hope and believed help was coming. The Bible says, “Be still, and know that I am God.” That doesn’t mean we stop caring. It means we stop trying to be in control. That verse isn’t just for people who live quiet lives. It’s for people like us. The ones who are tired. The ones who are hurting. The ones who’ve tried everything, and now… maybe it’s time to stop and sit for a while.

---

**[02-with-me-always-010]** · page 6 · 60w · _weary, anxious, trusting, resting_

We don’t know what that looks like for you. But we are learning that being still doesn’t mean we have failed. It just means we are learning to trust. Trust in the Lord. Trust that the Lord will take care of us. And maybe, when life feels too hard, trusting the Lord and being still is enough for today. 6

### Lesson 2: Follow Closely

**[02-with-me-always-012]** · page 7 · 99w · _striving, anxious, discouraged, confused_

We are sure there have been times in our lives when we have ran ahead of God. We have hastedly made decisions before we prayed. We have tried to fix things on our own. We tell ourselves we were being “proactive,” but really, we were impatient and maybe a little scared to wait. We lacked patience. We lacked trust. And then there were other times when we stopped walking altogether. We got stuck. Completely lost. We didn’t know where to go, so we just sat down, like a dog that refuses to move in the middle of a sidewalk.

---

**[02-with-me-always-013]** · page 7 · 81w · _trusting, peaceful, surrendered, resting_

But the times we remember best were the times we followed the Lord closely. Not perfectly; we are human after all. And not always happily. But we stayed near the Lord as best as we could. We didn’t try to control everything. We can’t anyway. We just walked, one step at a time, next to the One who knew the way. 7 Dogs don’t need to know where we’re going. They just need to know we know. That’s enough for them.

---

**[02-with-me-always-014]** · page 8 · 84w · _anxious, trusting, surrendered, hopeful_

Faith is like that. It’s not about having our own map, figuring out our destination, and then coming up with a plan to get there. Sure, we need to do our part, but faith is about knowing and trusting the One who’s leading. And us following. There was a woman who was going through something really difficult. She was homeless and sick, but she still came to church on Sundays. She said she felt safer there, even if it was just for an hour.

---

**[02-with-me-always-015]** · page 8 · 100w · _trusting, peaceful, loved, called_

One day, she was asked how she kept showing up with such peace. She said, “I just try to stay close to the Lord. God walks ahead of me. I just follow.” The Bible says, “My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me.” That’s from Jesus. He’s asking us to stay close to Him. To listen to His voice. To follow His lead. That’s what we see in good dogs, the ones who walk right beside us, matching our pace, keeping their eyes on us, trusting that wherever we’re going… it’s going to be okay.

---

**[02-with-me-always-016]** · page 8 · 71w · _anxious, trusting, peaceful, resting_

Don’t we want to live like that? To walk like that. Close to the Lord. Quietly. Trusting in the Lord. Faithfully. Not trying to rush off somewhere. Not running away. Just following the Lord. Right next to the Lord. 8 Because honestly, we don’t need to know everything about the road ahead. We just need to know the Lord knows the road ahead and that we are not walking alone. 9

### Lesson 3: Speak When It Matters

**[02-with-me-always-020]** · page 11 · 94w · _striving, called, trusting, surrendered_

There’s a verse that says, “Let your words be few.” Another says, “A gentle answer turns away anger.” Jesus Himself often stayed silent when people attacked Him. But when He did speak it was always on purpose. Always with truth. Always with love. That’s what we must learn. To stay quiet when words would only add noise. But to speak, clearly, purposefully, with love, when it helps someone else see what’s true. A good dog doesn’t bark to show off. A good dog barks to protect. To warn. To say, “Hey, something’s not right.”

### Lesson 4: Come When Called — *Learning to Return — No Matter How Far We've Wandered*

**[02-with-me-always-022]** · page 12 · 125w · _guilty, ashamed, restored, loved_

But when his name was called, even from way across the field, he’d pause. Sometimes he’d hesitate. Sometimes he’d give that look. Really? Right now? But then, every time, he’d come running. That’s the kind of heart we want to have with God. One that hears His voice, even when we’ve wandered far. A heart that turns back, even if it takes a second. The truth is, we have had seasons where we didn’t respond right away. Where we ignored the gentle nudge. Where we told ourselves we’d come back later. But the longer we waited, the harder it felt to return. Not because God had changed his mind or moved away; He is always waiting for us to return, with arms wide open. Always.

Not to shame us, but to bring us back to Him. Jesus once told a story about a lost sheep. It wandered off. He just embraces us with a love we will not fully understand this side of heaven. Dogs instinctively know something about that. Probably. They might wander. But when they hear their name, they come running. Let’s be like that. Because no matter how far we’ve gone, wouldn’t we rather be near Him than anywhere else?

### Lesson 5: Wag Anyway

**[02-with-me-always-026]** · page 14 · 95w · _weary, grieving, discouraged, hopeful_

Life can be challenging. Life can hurt. Not all the time, maybe, but often enough. Things go wrong. Bodies break down. Plans fall apart. People let us down. And yet, somehow, we’re still here. And if we’re still here, maybe there’s a way to show a little joy. A little hope. Even if it’s just a small wag. Many of us have experienced or are currently experiencing challenging situations. We are living in shelters. Or are at hospitals recovering. Some of us are living in tents or in our cars. Yet, we can still smile.

---

**[02-with-me-always-027]** · page 14 · 106w · _weary, grieving, joyful, grateful_

That doesn’t mean we pretend everything’s okay when it’s not. It doesn’t mean we fake a smile or hide how we feel. It just means… even here, even now, we can still be thankful. For something. Like a warm cup of coffee. A friend who visits us at the hospital. A place to sit, rest, and just breathe. A word from Scripture that reminds us that we are still loved.

---

**[02-with-me-always-028]** · page 15 · 107w · _weary, grieving, joyful, trusting_

Dogs wag when they’re happy, yes. But sometimes they wag when they’re hurting, too. They wag because they know that the person who is near them cares for them. Because they trust the one who is with them. That’s the kind of joy. Not a loud kind of joy. And it does not have to be perfect, at first. But it does need to be real. The Bible says, “Rejoice in the Lord always.” Impossible? Think of it as more like wagging. Even in the middle of all the mess, all the pain, we can be glad that the Lord is here with us through it all.

---

**[02-with-me-always-029]** · page 15 · 74w · _weary, grieving, joyful, loved_

That we have an appetite. Are able to taste good food and say, “Oh, that’s good.” Joy can live alongside pain. Even on the hardest days. Go ahead and wag anyway. Because the Lord is with us. 15

### Lesson 7: Stay With Me

**[02-with-me-always-036]** · page 18 · 109w · _grieving, lonely, loved, peaceful_

Dogs are good at just being near us. They don’t try to cheer us up with advice. They don’t leave when things get awkward or strange. They simply stay. A dog that quietly stays with us is a gift, especially when we’re hurting. I remember sitting beside someone in a hospital once. There wasn’t much I could do. There was absolutely nothing I could do. No way to make the situation better. No perfect words. Not one. Just a chair and a little bit of time. I sat. I stayed. I prayed in my heart. And when I got up to leave, he said quietly, “Thanks for being here.”

---

**[02-with-me-always-037]** · page 18 · 80w · _grieving, lonely, loved, forgotten_

Sometimes what people need most isn’t a solution. It’s not the right words or an action plan. It’s just someone who doesn’t walk away. 18 Jesus did that. He stayed with people who were hurting. He touched the ones everyone else avoided. He cried with His friends. He didn’t run from pain. Not their pain, and not ours. The Bible says, “The Lord is close to the brokenhearted.” It doesn’t say He fixes everything right away. It says He’s close.

---

**[02-with-me-always-038]** · page 19 · 121w · _lonely, grieving, loved, trusting_

Don’t we all want to be like that? Stay close when someone feels alone. Even if we can’t fix the situation. Even if we don’t know what to say. Even if we can’t do anything to make things better. Dogs don’t always understand what we’re going through. But they know when to stay close. That’s a kind of love. Not just showing up when it’s easy, but sticking around when it is difficult. We don’t have to have all the answers. We don’t need answers at all. When things get hard, it’s easy to disappear, but if we want to love we need to stick around. We need to stay, just like the Lord stays with us, especially through hard times.

Especially when things don’t go the way we think they should. When things run low. Our checking accounts. Food in the fridge. Time. Energy. Hope. Especially when I’ve been waiting a while.

One morning, he was asked how he stayed so calm. He said, “I’ve gone to bed hungry plenty of times. But God hasn’t forgotten me.” That is trust. In the Bible, Jesus tells His followers not to worry about food or clothes. He says the birds don’t store up food, and the flowers don’t work for beauty. But God feeds and clothes them anyway. Then He says, “Aren’t you worth more than they are?” God tells us we are. It’s not always easy to believe that. But don’t we believe what Jesus is saying is true? God gives us what we need. Maybe not everything we ask for, but enough to keep going. Not as soon as possible, but in God’s perfect timing. He gives us enough to remind us we’re not forgotten. And sometimes much more than enough.

A trusting heart that trusts the Hand that feeds us. We are all learning. To worry less. To ask simply. To wait with hope. Because even when we don’t see it yet, we believe the bowl will be filled by our good Lord.

### Lesson 9: Sleep In Peace

**[02-with-me-always-044]** · page 23 · 81w · _anxious, afraid, resting, trusting_

Dogs can sleep almost anywhere. Haven’t we seen them stretched out on sidewalks? Curled up in noisy shelters. Tucked under tables during thunderstorms. Sometimes they sleep with one eye open, but still they sleep. And I’ve wondered how they do that. How can they rest in places that feel so uncertain? Part of the answer is simple: they trust who they’re with. They know someone is watching over them. And that’s enough. More than enough for them to sleep in peace.

---

**[02-with-me-always-045]** · page 23 · 114w · _anxious, afraid, weary, resting_

There are dogs that sometimes sleep at our feet. Whenever we move, they lift their heads. Just a little, to see if we were still there. Then they would sigh and settle back down. As long as we were near, they could sleep. Haven’t there been been plenty of nights in our lives when we just couldn’t fall sleep? It might have been stress. Fear. Pain. Pain in our hearts. Pain in our bodies. Thoughts that run wild after the lights go out. The body might have been tired, but the mind kept spinning. It’s hard to rest when the world feels like it’s falling apart. Or when our own lives feel too uncertain.

---

**[02-with-me-always-046]** · page 23 · 108w · _anxious, afraid, resting, trusting_

23 But in the Psalms, there’s a verse that says, “I will lie down and sleep in peace, for You alone, Lord, make me dwell in safety.” It doesn’t say the Lord made the world a peaceful place. It doesn’t say the problems went away. It just says: the Lord is with us. We are safe. We can rest now. Dogs understand this in their own way. They don’t control much. They don’t know what tomorrow holds. But when they know they’re cared for, they are able to sleep. Not because their lives are perfect. But because they believe they’re protected and taken care of by their person.

---

**[02-with-me-always-047]** · page 24 · 90w · _anxious, weary, resting, surrendered_

Rest is not just something the body needs. It’s something the heart needs, too. And sometimes, the bravest thing we can do at the end of a hard day is to close our eyes and trust that God will take care of whatever we are worrying about. So, let’s stop carrying everything and put our worries down, at the foot of our Lord. Let’s stop rehearsing all the what-ifs. Instead, let’s breathe. Physically and spiritually. And be still. And sleep, like someone who is safely loved. Because we are loved.

### Lesson 10: Leave The Past — *Learning to Move On from What We Can't Change*

They don’t carry their regrets. They don’t replay it. They leave the past behind. And they keep walking. Don’t we all wish we were more like that? We carry things. Sometimes more than we should. Old regrets. Words we said and wish we hadn’t. Moments we replay over and over, trying to fix what’s already finished. But the past doesn’t change, no matter how long we carry it with us. And the more we stare at it, the harder it is to see what’s ahead.

---

**[02-with-me-always-050]** · page 25 · 107w · _guilty, ashamed, forgiven, restored_

To trust that God’s mercy and grace really does mean we are forgiven and loved. And that even when we mess up, God doesn’t walk away. He still calls us with open arms.

And when they arrive, they don’t knock. They don’t ask if it’s okay. They come in, wag their tail, curl up in their bed, and fall asleep like they never left. That’s a kind of trust we see often with dogs, but us? Not as often. Life can feel like wandering. Like walking through places that aren’t home. He will take us in with open arms and a smile that says, “Welcome. You made it. I love you.”
