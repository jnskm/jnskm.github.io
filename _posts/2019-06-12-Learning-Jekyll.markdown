I want to learn Jekyll. There are a few reasons why.

First, I wanted to see if I can learn something other than WordPress, which I have been using since 2008. WP is powerful, but it is also complicated. I still don't quite understand PHP and mySQL. And recently I was not able to update to a new WP version because the PHP version used on my host was 5.5.x and it needed 5.6.x. I didn't know how to update PHP version on my host, so I started looking elsewhere to blog. (I did eventually find out how to update the PHP version. I just needed to get to the particular subdirectory where WP was installed to upgrade the PHP version. I wasn't that hard once I figured this little requirement out.)

Second, I liked the idea of my blog not having to need a database. A database-free blog? Sure, why not. Simpler is better I think.

Third, I wanted to get away from having to pay for hosting. This won't happen since I have a couple of sites, but I think it will be good practice if I want to prepare for when I want to simplify my online presence in the form of websites and blogs.
