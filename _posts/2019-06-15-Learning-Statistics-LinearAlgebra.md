I've been learning statistics and linear algebra. Mostly through Kahn Academy, YouTube, Coursera and a couple of other online sources. It's been pretty great. When I'm learning something and I get stuck, I just open up another tab and search for that particular subject matter and learn that. Afterwards, I go back to my original learning and continue. There's potential for jumping all over the place if I don't have discipline and I've done that a little already, but with KA and Coursera it's really easy to see your progress.

The ultimate goal for me is to learn how to program machine learning. I would like build robots that are able to identify the type of plant, figure out soil conditions and then recommend what needs to be done next to make sure the plant is getting all of its nutrients. Perhaps even connect to a water source to water it when necessary. When to harvest. When to seed. How to cook even. Other robots to determin water quality levels, air quality. Figure out how to do laundry. Dust levels on carpets and recommend vacuuming. Or make Roomba go to work. All sorts of things that make living more automated. When to make investments and what type of investments. What type of food to buy depending on the season and location.

What type clothing to buy that's reliable, durable, a good value, and good for the environment and for the people who are involved in manufacturing the clothing. I'm thinking of robots that can make recommendations for almost everything that we need. Figure out when we have the most energy and what type of foods we need to eat based on our environment, family history, and DNA testing.

Home defence rebots. Sentinels. Communicators. Door defence augmentation. Active defence? Work with other people in the neighborhood to alert them and request help. Everything.

Continously learn from books, written by really smart people who know what they are talking about. Will limit knowledge gained from the internet unless it is a vetted source. For instance, financial information from sources like the Wall Street Journal and the Financial Times. Western standard medical information from places like WebMD. I think identifying reliable sources of information on the internet might be an important capability for a learning robot. What makes a source reliable? It might be simple: look at the author and determine if s/he is from a reputable institution and has relavant experience. Crosscheck. Read his comments and what she posts and says on social media.
