Because of the way the brain works, I need to repeat something over and over, and over again for something new to stick. Or more accurately for something new I am trying to learn, for that to chunk and then to be stored in long term memory. But I can't repeat right after one another. I need some time for my brain to process (chunk) what I am learning and that takes time. Sometimes it takes a nap but more often it takes an entire night of sleeping.

So there is really no way to rapidly learn something new. You need the daily cycle of learning and then sleeping for what you are learning to become part of your long-term memory. And when that happens what you are learning becomes something like second nature. You become extremely proficient at it.

Now chunking just means a grouping of things. Let's take driving for example. Put the key in. Make sure the car is in park. Press the brake and then turn the key all the way clockwise until the engine starts. This I don't think about on a step-by-step basis because it's chunked in my long-term memory and it is more or less second nature, automatic. That is why I am really good at doing it. But then there are other, many other chunks, when it comes to driving a car. And to get all those chunks into long-term memory takes a long time and a lot of practice.

So that's what I will do with Jekyll. I'm still learning the "front matter". It will take a long time for me to get proficient and another long time for me to get really good at it and then another long time for me to get to a level where it is like breathing. Automatic.
